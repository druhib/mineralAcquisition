What is cobalt ?

Cobalt is a transition metal with increasing demand due to its ferromagnetic properties, which make it a critical component of efficient batteries and superalloys [1]. Pure cobalt metal is somewhat reactive with oxygen, so it naturally occurs embedded in several complex ores, such as sulfides, sulfarsenides, cobaltiferous iron sulfides, and arsenides. [2][3]

Where was cobalt discovered?

Cobalt metal was discovered in 1730 in Stockholm, Sweden by George Brandt [4]. However, cobalt ore has been used for its distinctive blues pigment for thousands of years, originating in Egypt [5].
