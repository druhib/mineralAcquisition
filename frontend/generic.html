Lithium

What is lithium?

Lithium is an alkali metal. Alkali metals are found embedded in igneous rock, as these metals are highly reactive in air at standard temperature and pressure. Lithium has been recovered from igneous rock called pegmatite, which has large interlocking crystals.

How is Lithium Mined

Greenbushes Mine, Australia

The Greenbushes Mine is a hard-rock mining site that started off in 1888 producing tin. Since 1983, the mine has developed into a leading producer of lithium ore. Today Greenbushes supplies 20% of the world's lithium by mining spodumene.
